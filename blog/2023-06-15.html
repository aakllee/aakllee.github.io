How to Close the Sidebar▮▮▮▮

2023-06-15

Hello! This marks the first "post" (more of a test than anything) on my site. Cue confetti cannons.

If you are using a landscape oriented screen, you may have noticed the large black box taking up a large portion of the viewport.

Now either you don't care what it says and have proceeded to completely ignore it, or you have read it and now that it's done you want it out of your face.

But lo! There is no big red X—no close, no collapse!

So what to do then?

A simple solution is to inspect element and delete the <aside>...</aside>. Firefox's element picker is especially convenient here (Ctrl+Shift+c).

You might also choose to force portrait mode by editing the @media check for screen size to be larger than your screen.

Neither of these, however, persist across pages or reloads. So what now?

We could use uBlock Origin's element picker to do much the same thing, but this time persisting. This, however, requires the use of a third-party extension—and we don't like those here.

Firefox has a css file somewhere in ~/.mozilla where you can specify css to be injected into websites. You could write such a rule for this site, and have display: hidden;

Point is, doing such a thing server-side would make the page load all the more slower for people on potato devices and therefore less accessible to much of the world. Thus I offload the effort to the client-side devices.

Call it a lesson in hacker spirit (as in hacky solutions (not cybercrime)).
